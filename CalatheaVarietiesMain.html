Calathea Varieties

The Calathea genus, often referred to as a "prayer plant" due to its relation to the Marantaceae family, is highly prized among houseplant enthusiasts for its extraordinarily vibrant and decorative foliage. Native to the rainforest floors of Central and South America, these tropical beauties are characterized by their wide, colorfully patterned leaves that can feature shades of green, pink, white, and purple. One of their most fascinating traits is nyctinasty, the ability of the leaves to fold up at night and unfurl again during the day in response to light, mimicking hands in prayer. To successfully grow a Calathea indoors, you must mimic its jungle origins by providing bright, indirect light, consistently moist (but never soggy) soil, and critically, high humidity. They are also notably sensitive to the minerals found in regular tap water, often showing browning or crispy leaf edges if not given filtered or distilled water.

Species

  • Primary Species

    • Common Name: Widely Known Scientific Name: Current Accepted Scientific Name: Notes

    • Rattlesnake Plant: Calathea lancifolia: Goeppertia insignis: Known for long, wavy, spotted leaves.

    • Peacock Plant: Calathea makoyana: Goeppertia makoyana: Oval leaves with feather-like markings.

    • Zebra Plant: Calathea zebrina: Goeppertia zebrina: Velvety leaves with distinct dark stripes.

    • Round-Leaf Calathea: Calathea orbifolia: Geoppertia orbifolia: Large, round leaves with silvery-green stripes.

    • Pin-stripe Calathea: Calathea ornata: Geoppertia ornata: Dark green leaves with fine pink or white stripes.

    • Jungle Velvet: Calathea warscewiczii: Geoppertia warscewiczii: Velvety, dark green leaves with red/purple undersides.

    • Rose-Painted Calathea: Calathea roseopicta: Goeppertia roseopicta: Varieties include 'Dottie' and 'Medallion'.

    • Eternal Flame: Calathea crocata: Geoppertia crocata: Popular for its bright, orange-yellow flowers.

    • Furry Feather Calathea: Calathea rufibarba: Goeppertia rufibarba: Long, narrow leaves with a fuzzy/velvety texture.

    • Network/Mosaic Plant: Calathea musaica: Goeppertia kegeljanii: Leaves with an intricate, tile-like pattern.

    • Brazilian Star Calathea: Calathea loeseneri: Goeppertia loeseneri: Known for its star-shaped flowers and striped leaves.
    • Thai Beauty: Calathea louisae: Goeppertia louisae: Various cultivars, often with lime green or white variegated leaves.
  • Cultivars

    • Common Name: Widely Known Scientific Name: Current Accepted Scientific Name: Notes

    • 'White Fusion': Calathea 'White Fusion': Goeppertia 'White Fusion': Stunning variegation of white, cream, and green.
    • 'Triostar': Compact, ruffled fronds.
  • True Calathea Plants

    • Common Name: Current Accepted Scientific Name: Notes

    • Cigar Plant: Calathea lutea: Has large leaves often used for wrapping food.
    • Rattlesnake Calathea: Calathea crotalifera: Named for the rattle-like seed pod.
    • No Recognized Common Name: Calathea casupito: A tropical plant with large, colorful, patterned foliage.
