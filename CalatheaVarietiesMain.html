Calathea Varieties

The Calathea genus, often referred to as a "prayer plant" due to its relation to the Marantaceae family, is highly prized among houseplant enthusiasts for its extraordinarily vibrant and decorative foliage. Native to the rainforest floors of Central and South America, these tropical beauties are characterized by their wide, colorfully patterned leaves that can feature shades of green, pink, white, and purple. One of their most fascinating traits is nyctinasty, the ability of the leaves to fold up at night and unfurl again during the day in response to light, mimicking hands in prayer. To successfully grow a Calathea indoors, you must mimic its jungle origins by providing bright, indirect light, consistently moist (but never soggy) soil, and critically, high humidity. They are also notably sensitive to the minerals found in regular tap water, often showing browning or crispy leaf edges if not given filtered or distilled water.
