Autumn Fern (Dryopteris erythrosora)

The Autumn Fern (Dryopteris erythrosora) is a striking shade-loving plant known for its coppery-red new fronds that mature into deep green, creating a dynamic, seasonal display. Native to East Asia, it thrives in moist, well-draining soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it ideal for woodland gardens or shaded indoor corners. Its leathery, arching fronds offer year-round interest, and it’s prized for its resilience and low-maintenance nature. With its warm-toned foliage and graceful form, the Autumn Fern adds texture and color contrast to both naturalistic and curated botanical spaces.

Care Guide

  • Light:
    Prefers partial to full shade. Avoid direct sun, which can scorch its coppery new fronds.
  • Watering:
    Keep soil consistently moist but not soggy. Water when the top layer begins to dry.
  • Humidity:
    Enjoys moderate to high humidity. Ideal for shaded patios or misted indoor corners.
  • Temperature:
    Thrives in 55–75°F (13–24°C). Hardy in USDA zones 5–9 for outdoor planting.
  • Soil:
    Rich, well-draining soil with organic matter. Mulch helps retain moisture and regulate temperature.
  • Fertilizer:
    Feed lightly in spring with a balanced, slow-release fertilizer. Avoid overfeeding.
  • Pruning:
    Remove faded or damaged fronds at the base to encourage fresh growth.
  • Repotting:
    If indoors, repot every 2–3 years or when rootbound. Choose a container with good drainage.
  • Toxicity:
    Non-toxic to pets, safe for homes with cats and dogs.
