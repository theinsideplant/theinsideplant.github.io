Bird’s Nest Ferns

Bird’s Nest Ferns (Asplenium nidus and its relatives) are tropical epiphytes known for their lush, rosette-shaped foliage that resembles a nest—hence the name. Native to Southeast Asia, Polynesia, and parts of Australia, these ferns thrive in humid, shaded environments and are often found growing on trees in the wild. Their strap-like fronds emerge from a central crown and can vary from smooth and glossy to deeply ruffled or spiraled, depending on the cultivar. Unlike many ferns, Bird’s Nest Ferns prefer indirect light and consistent moisture, making them ideal for indoor settings where they add a dramatic, architectural touch. With varieties like ‘Crissie’, ‘Hurricane’, and ‘Victoria’, they offer a range of textures and forms that suit both minimalist and jungle-inspired interiors. Their air-purifying qualities and low-maintenance nature make them a favorite among plant enthusiasts and designers alike.

Care Guide

  • Primary Species
  • Asplenium nidus L.
    Most widely cultivated; broad, strap-like fronds.
  • Asplenium antiquum Makino
    Narrower fronds; often confused with A. nidus
  • Asplenium australasicum(J.Sm.)
    Leathery fronds; native to Australia

  • Cultivars (Named Varieties of A.nidus or A.antiquum)
  • Asplenium nidus 'Crissie':
    Deeply split, frilly leaf tips.
  • Asplenium antiquum 'Victoria':
    Compact, ruffled fronds.
  • Asplenium nidus 'Hurricane':
    Spiral-shaped, swirling fronds.
  • Asplenium nidus 'Cobra':
    Twisted, undulating fronds.
  • Asplenium nidus 'Fimbriatum':
    Finely fringed leaf margins.
  • Asplenium nidus 'Osaka':
    Glossy, arching fronds.
  • Asplenium nidus 'Leslie':
    Compact, rippled fronds.
  • Asplenium nidus 'Ruffled':
    Deeply undulating fronds.
  • Asplenium nidus 'Nidus Avis':
    Rare cultivar with upright, thick fronds.
